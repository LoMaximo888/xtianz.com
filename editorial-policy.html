EDITORIAL POLICY

TRUST + TRANSPARENCY

How XTIANZ creates, reviews, and updates content.

Editorial purpose

XTIANZ exists to make complex AI systems easier to understand through original frameworks, plain-language explanations, and practical questions readers can use.

Sources

Fast-changing company, product, market, and policy facts should be grounded in primary sources when available: official documentation, investor relations, regulatory filings, research papers, or direct company announcements. Secondary reporting may add context but should not replace the primary record.

AI assistance

AI tools may help organize research, test structure, generate alternatives, or identify gaps. They are not treated as authoritative sources. Published content is manually reviewed, edited, and checked for unsupported claims.

Freshness

Evergreen explainers are reviewed when the underlying technology or company status changes materially. Market-sensitive pages display review context and direct readers to official sources for current prices, filings, and product details.

Corrections

Material factual errors should be corrected promptly. Readers can submit a correction request through the Contact page or the public GitHub issue workflow.

Independence

Editorial recommendations are based on usefulness and relevance. Any future referral, sponsorship, or affiliate relationship will be disclosed near the relevant link.

Local-news source policy

The DMV Pulse section is a source navigator, not a replacement for local journalism. XTIANZ links directly to The Burn, Loudoun Now, official Loudoun County pages, WTOP, FFXnow, ARLnow, NBC4 Washington, WUSA9, and other original publishers. Headlines and full reporting remain on those sites.

When XTIANZ publishes original regional analysis, it separates commentary from sourced facts, links to primary documents when available, and includes a visible review date.
