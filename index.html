PROTOCOLRELEASE CANDIDATE

MCP 2025-11-25 remains stable; 2026-07-28 is the next release candidate.

Production teams should implement the stable protocol while testing the forthcoming stateless core, extensions, Tasks, MCP Apps, authorization hardening, and deprecation policy in controlled environments.

Why it matters
Separating stable requirements from release-candidate behavior prevents accidental compatibility and governance debt.
What would invalidate this
The official project publishes 2026-07-28 as final or changes the planned scope.
Official release-candidate brief ↗
OPERATIONSHIGH CONFIDENCE

Agent quality is increasingly an evaluation and observability discipline.

Model intelligence is only one component. Production reliability depends on traceable tool calls, task-level evaluations, guardrails, human escalation, rollback, and cost monitoring.

Why it matters
Teams can optimize actual failure modes instead of judging systems from polished demonstrations.
What would invalidate this
Repeatable evidence that unmanaged autonomy outperforms governed systems across consequential workflows.
Primary engineering source ↗
INFRASTRUCTUREHIGH CONFIDENCE

The DMV is a visible test case for AI infrastructure constraints.

Loudoun County is actively coordinating utilities, residents, planners, and data-center operators around electrical infrastructure, land use, noise, energy storage, and community impact.

Why it matters
AI scaling ultimately depends on power, permitting, construction, fiber, cooling, and public acceptance.
What would invalidate this
Material reductions in compute demand or infrastructure requirements that remove local capacity pressure.
Official Loudoun update ↗
