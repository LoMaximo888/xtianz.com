PROTOCOLMEDIUM CONFIDENCE

MCP 2026-07-28 is a release candidate, not the production baseline.

Evidence: The official MCP project describes the release candidate as adding a stateless core, extensions, Tasks, MCP Apps, authorization hardening, and deprecation policy.

Engineering action: Keep production compatibility on 2025-11-25; test beta SDKs and migration paths separately.

Invalidation: The official project publishes the specification as final or materially changes the release.

AGENT OPERATIONSHIGH CONFIDENCE

Evaluation, tracing, guardrails, and recovery define production maturity.

Evidence: Current agent-engineering guidance emphasizes tracing, evaluations, orchestration, and guardrails as core operational capabilities.

Engineering action: Gate autonomy on measurable task success, tool-call accuracy, safe failure, cost, and human escalation.

Invalidation: Sustained field evidence demonstrates that comparable controls do not improve reliability or risk.

Primary source ↗
INFRASTRUCTUREHIGH CONFIDENCE

Power and community coordination are becoming first-order AI constraints.

Evidence: Loudoun County’s Electrical Infrastructure Working Group includes utilities, data-center operators, staff, homeowners, and community organizations.

Engineering action: Include energy, site, network, water, permitting, and community dependencies in capacity plans.

Invalidation: AI compute demand or infrastructure intensity declines enough to remove local capacity pressure.

Official source ↗
RISKHIGH CONFIDENCE

AI risk management remains a lifecycle operating model.

Evidence: NIST’s AI RMF and Generative AI Profile organize risk work across governance, mapping, measurement, and management.

Engineering action: Tie risk controls to system ownership, evidence, release gates, monitoring, and incident response.

Invalidation: A materially different cross-industry standard replaces lifecycle-based governance.

Official source ↗
