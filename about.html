About XTIANZ

XTIANZ is an independent AI and technology publication with a local point of view from the Washington metropolitan region.

The site connects four layers that are often discussed separately: AI capability, enterprise deployment, physical infrastructure, and market economics. The DMV section adds regional context from Loudoun County, Northern Virginia, Washington, D.C., and Maryland.

Editorial focus

  • AI agents, Model Context Protocol, security, governance, and enterprise operations.
  • MANGOS: Meta, Anthropic, Nvidia, Google, OpenAI, and SpaceX.
  • AI infrastructure, data centers, power, networking, cloud platforms, and technology markets.
  • DMV and LoCo source navigation, with direct links to original local publishers and official agencies.

Why local news is included

Loudoun and Northern Virginia are important technology-infrastructure regions. Local development, grid planning, transportation, land use, public services, and data-center policy can provide practical context for the global AI story.

How XTIANZ handles local reporting

XTIANZ does not copy or summarize local articles as if they were its own reporting. The DMV Pulse page describes what each source covers and sends readers to the original publisher for current headlines and full reporting.

How articles are maintained

Original guides use visible manual review dates. Fast-changing facts should be checked against primary sources, official company pages, and public documents. Readers can suggest corrections through the Contact page.
